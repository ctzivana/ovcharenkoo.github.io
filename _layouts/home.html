Currently I am a PhD student at King Abdullah University of Science and Technology (KAUST) in Seismic Modeling and Inversion group run by Prof. Daniel Peter. My research interests are: geophysics, numerical modeling, seismic inversion, and machine learning.

I grew up in Moscow where since 2009 have been studying Earth Science at Lomonosov Moscow State University. During 5.5 years of M.Sc program I was conducting research on natural stress-state reconstruction at the lab of tectonophysics run by Dr. Yu.L. Rebetsky at the Schmidt Institute of Physics of the Earth of the Russian Academy of Sciences.

In 2014 I got accepted for the International Master of Research in Exploration Geophysics (GPX) at the Institut de Physique du Globe de Paris (IPGP) in Paris. There, I was working with Prof. Nobuaki Fuji and Dr. Roland Martin (CNRS) developing an accurate finite difference operator for synthetic seismogram calculation in areas with sharp lithological interfaces.

Aside from that I just like learning new things, doing sports and playing guitar.
