Currently I am a PhD candidate at King Abdullah University of Science and Technology (KAUST) in Seismic Modeling and Inversion group run by Prof. Daniel Peter. I am broadly interested in numerical methods in global and exploration geophysics, which includes but not limited to:

  • Seismic wave simulation
  • Full-Waveform Inversion
  • Natural stress state reconstruction
  • HPC
  • Machine Learning
My PhD research is focused on applying ML techniques to geophysical problems.

Bio

I grew up in Moscow and received my M.Sc degree in Earth Science from Lomonosov Moscow State University in 2014. During the M.Sc program my research was focused on natural stress-state reconstruction under the supervision of Dr. Yu.L. Rebetsky in the tectonophysics lab at the Schmidt Institute of Physics of the Earth of the Russian Academy of Sciences.

In 2015 I got International Master of Research in Exploration Geophysics (GPX) degree from the Institut de Physique du Globe de Paris (IPGP, Paris VII) in Paris. There, I worked with Prof. Nobuaki Fuji and Dr. Roland Martin (CNRS) on accurate finite difference operators for synthetic seismogram calculation in areas with sharp lithological interfaces.

Besides research activities, I am taking part in life of KAUST Venture Fund. Aside from that, I like learning languages, good food, martial arts and playing guitar.
