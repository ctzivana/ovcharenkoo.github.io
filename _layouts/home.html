Currently I am a PhD candidate at King Abdullah University of Science and Technology (KAUST) in Seismic Modeling and Inversion group run by Prof. Daniel Peter. My research interests are: geophysics, numerical modeling and machine learning.

I grew up in Moscow and received my M.Sc degree in Earth Science from Lomonosov Moscow State University in 2014. During the M.Sc program my research was focused on natural stress-state reconstruction under the supervision of Dr. Yu.L. Rebetsky in the tectonophysics lab at the Schmidt Institute of Physics of the Earth of the Russian Academy of Sciences.

In 2015 received International Master of Research in Exploration Geophysics (GPX) degree from the Institut de Physique du Globe de Paris (IPGP) in Paris. There, I worked with Prof. Nobuaki Fuji and Dr. Roland Martin (CNRS) on accurate finite difference operators for synthetic seismogram calculation in areas with sharp lithological interfaces.

Besides research activities, I like learning new things, good food, martial arts and playing guitar.
